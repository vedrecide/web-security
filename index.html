Implementing SSL

SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) is a protocol designed to secure and encrypt data transferred over the internet between a web browser and a web server. It ensures that sensitive information, such as login credentials, payment details, and personal data, remains private and cannot be intercepted by malicious third parties.

SSL is linked to HTTPS (Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure) through the use of SSL/TLS (Transport Layer Security) certificates. When a website uses HTTPS, it means that the communication between the user's browser and the website's server is encrypted using SSL/TLS protocols. This encryption helps protect the integrity and confidentiality of data transmitted over the internet.

Encryption

Encryption is like putting your message in a secret code before sending it to someone. Imagine you have a secret note you want to send to your friend. Instead of sending it as it is (where anyone could read it), you change each letter in the note to a different letter or symbol according to a secret rule only you and your friend know. When your friend gets the note, they change the letters back to read the original message.

Authentication

Authentication is like proving who you are before you can enter a secret club or use a special website. Imagine you have a membership card with your name and picture on it. When you show this card at the club's door or the website's login page, they check it to make sure it's really you. If it matches, they let you in or give you access to the website.
